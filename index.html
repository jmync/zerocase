Professional Summary

I started my career as a web developer, which gave me a solid foundation in software development and how systems are built. After that, I moved into quality assurance and spent several years working in manual testing, automation, and quality engineering.

I've worn a lot of hats over the years — managing delivery, designing systems, building automation, and leading QA. I worked closely with both developers and business teams, creating test strategies, designing automation frameworks, improving workflows, and guiding team processes. This multi-hat experience taught me how to connect technical and business needs, collaborate across teams, and adapt quickly to whatever a project needed most.

As my experience grew, I became more involved in workflow automation and AI-assisted solutions. I worked on automating processes across different systems and building AI-integrated workflows using n8n.

Now, my mindset has shifted. I see myself more as an AI-augmented developer — someone who combines development, quality engineering, automation, and AI tools to design and build reliable systems. My experience taught me how to look at systems as a whole: what needs to be built, how it should work, and how to make sure it delivers a good experience for users.
